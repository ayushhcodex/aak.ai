# 🤝 AAK-AI: Conference Networking & Growth Guide

As a student-led startup, your primary goal at this conference isn't just to show off your software—it's to **validate your idea, find data partners, and identify your first pilot customers.** 

Textile professionals know the physical materials; you know the AI. Your goal is to figure out where their biggest pain points are and how AIMATRY can solve them.

Here is a strategic list of questions tailored to the different types of professionals you will meet today:

---

### 🧪 1. Textile Research Scientists & Lab Heads
*These are the people running tests all day. They have the data your AI needs to learn.*

**Your Goal:** Understand their pain points in R&D and figure out how to get access to their historical testing data.
* "In your lab, which property (e.g., flame resistance, tensile strength) is the hardest to predict before actually manufacturing the sample?"
* "How many physical iterations does it typically take your team to finalize a new protective fabric blend?"
* "We are training our AI to predict standard test outcomes (like ISO 11612). If an AI could pre-screen materials and eliminate the worst 80% of candidates before you ever touch a loom, how much time/money would that save your lab?"
* **The "Ask":** "We are looking for academic partners to pilot our software. If you feed the AI your historical, anonymized test data, we can build a custom predictive model specifically for your lab. Is this something your department would explore?"

### 🏭 2. Industry Executives & Manufacturers (CEOs, Product Managers)
*They care about cost, speed-to-market, and supply chains. They don't care about the AI itself; they care about what it saves them.*

**Your Goal:** Learn about commercialization bottlenecks and pitch AIMATRY as a cost-saving tool.
* "When a client (like the military or fire department) asks for a new protective garment, what takes the longest in the development cycle?"
* "How much does it typically cost to prototype a completely new high-performance fabric from scratch?"
* "Are you currently facing pressure to substitute traditional materials (like PFAS/Teflon) with sustainable alternatives? How are you handling the R&D for that?"
* **The "Ask":** "We help manufacturers cut their prototyping costs by simulating the performance of blends virtually. Could we schedule a 15-minute call next week to show you how our platform could integrate into your current R&D pipeline?"

### 🎖️ 3. Defense & Government Officials (Procurement, Standards)
*They care about strict compliance, reliability, and domestic manufacturing (Make in India).*

**Your Goal:** Understand the specific standards they demand and position your startup as a sovereign, indigenous tech solution.
* "What is the biggest operational gap you see in the current protective gear issued to our personnel today?"
* "When evaluating a new material for defense procurement, what is the most common reason a fabric fails the qualification tests?"
* "We notice a heavy reliance on imported proprietary fibers (like Kevlar or Nomex). Is there a drive to develop indigenous Indian alternatives? How can predictive AI accelerate that?"
* **The "Ask":** "As an entirely student-led, indigenous startup developing AI for Indian defense materials, what is the proper channel for us to apply for defense innovation grants (like iDEX) or research incubation?"

### 📖 4. Academics & Professors
*They care about publications, theory, and pushing boundaries.*

**Your Goal:** Seek mentorship and keep up with cutting-edge materials.
* "What do you think is the most overhyped 'smart material' right now, and what is currently flying under the radar that we should train our AI on?"
* "We are currently using heuristics and published literature to score our materials. In your opinion, what is the best computational method for simulating polymer degradation over time?"
* **The "Ask":** "We are students looking to bridge the gap between computer science and material physics. Would you be open to being an informal advisor to our startup as we move toward commercializing this?"

---

### 💡 General Tips for the Team Today:
1. **Listen 80%, Speak 20%:** Let them talk about their problems. The more they complain about their current processes, the more you learn about what your AI needs to fix.
2. **Take Immediate Notes:** After a good conversation, write their name, company, and biggest pain point on the back of their business card immediately. 
3. **Be Proud You Are Students:** Don't hide it. Industry professionals *love* helping motivated students. Use the line: *"We are a student-led AI startup, and we'd love your expert advice on..."* It is the ultimate icebreaker.
4. **Follow Up:** A conversation today is useless without an email tomorrow. 

Good luck out there. You have a highly professional, visually stunning product backing you up!
